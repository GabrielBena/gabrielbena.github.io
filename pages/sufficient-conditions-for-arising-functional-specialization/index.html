+ # Structural vs Functional Modularity

  + **Modularity** is an essential concept in studying networks, both real and artificial. This concept is really twofold, composed of a functional and structural definition. As we'll see later on, both aspects are very much related, but it remains that functional modularity is often less studied and overall less understood than its structural counterpart.

  + We want to understand why specialization arises (as it seems to do, the brain is not homogeneous in structure or function), but also why it's understandble that it doesn't always, or at least in a more nuanced way.

+ # Measuring Funcspec

  + The way we measure functional specialization is by retraining agents to perform a particular subtasks, after being trained on another, usually broader or multiple tasks. This approach that seems quite simple is actually grounded in a theoretical information point of view : the **Information-Bottleneck**
